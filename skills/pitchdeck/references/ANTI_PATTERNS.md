# Anti-Patterns: What Makes a Pitch Deck Look Amateur or AI-Generated

## AI-Slop Indicators (2026)
These patterns now signal "generated by AI in 5 minutes" and actively hurt credibility:

1. **Sterile, AI-perfect typography** — uniform, computer-generated fonts without personality or warmth. 2026 favors human-crafted, expressive type.
2. **Midjourney/DALL-E stock imagery** — glossy, over-rendered photos of "diverse professionals" or product mockups. Investors spot these instantly.
3. **Template-obvious designs** — recognizable output from Gamma.app, Tome, Beautiful.ai, or SlidesAI. Every VC has seen hundreds.
4. **ChatGPT voice in copy** — "In today's rapidly evolving landscape...", "leveraging cutting-edge AI to...", "Our revolutionary platform seamlessly..."
5. **Inconsistent visual language** — different styles per slide (hallmark of AI-assembled decks)
6. **Perfect symmetry everywhere** — AI defaults to centered, balanced layouts. Intentional asymmetry signals human design.
7. **Generic color schemes** — especially purple gradients on white backgrounds
8. **Overuse of single accent color without system** — 2026 expects full color systems, not one-note accent

## Design Patterns Now Considered Dated

| Pattern | Why Dead | Replaced By |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Gradient blob backgrounds | Ubiquitous 2019-2022, screams "template" | Subtle noise textures, soft-glow gradients |
| Pure white (#FFFFFF) backgrounds | Too sterile | Off-white (#FAFAF8, #F0EEE9) |
| Pure black (#000000) text | Too harsh | Near-black (#1A1A1A, #1F2937) |
| Blue-only palettes (#2563EB) | Every SaaS deck 2015-2022 | Indigo, teal, warm neutrals |
| Montserrat / Poppins headlines | Overused to invisibility | Satoshi, Syne, Fraunces, Clash Display |
| Bullet-point-heavy slides | Density = laziness | One stat per slide, visual storytelling |
| TAM/SAM/SOM concentric circles | Identical in every deck | Bottom-up market sizing with real data |
| Pie charts | Hard to read, outdated | Horizontal bar charts |
| 3D chart effects | Distort proportions, amateur | Flat, clean, minimal charts |
| Stock photos (handshakes, teams) | Inauthentically generic | Real photos, product screenshots, CSS art |
| "Questions?" final slide | Wastes most memorable position | Strong CTA with specific ask |
| Slide numbers visible | Unnecessary clutter | Clean, no pagination |
| Drop shadows on everything | 2018 depth effect | 1px borders or no elevation |
| Complex org charts | Nobody cares | Team slide with photos + credentials |
| Dual-axis charts | Confusing, low trust | Separate charts or simplified |

## Fonts: DO NOT USE as Display
Inter (oversaturated), Roboto, Arial, system fonts, Calibri

## Colors: DO NOT USE
- `#6366F1` as sole accent (generic Tailwind indigo)
- Purple-on-white gradients
- Rainbow color coding in charts
- Neon green on black (unless Terminal Green preset)

## Copy Anti-Patterns
- "Revolutionizing the industry" — show, don't claim
- "AI-powered" as primary differentiator — "the more a founder says AI, the less AI the company likely uses"
- "No competition" — instant credibility loss
- Buzzword density > insight density
- More than 30 words body text per slide

## The 2026 Counter-Movement: "Imperfect by Design"
Canva's 2026 trend report identified a backlash against AI perfection. Decks that show human craft — hand-drawn elements, organic shapes, intentional asymmetry, editorial typography — now signal authenticity and build trust. Imperfection is the new premium.
