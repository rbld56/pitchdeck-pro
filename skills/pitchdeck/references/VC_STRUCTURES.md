# VC-Recommended Pitch Deck Structures

Reference guide for the pitchdeck-pro plugin. Defines three canonical VC structures
plus a 2026 extended variant with mandatory additions reflecting current investor expectations.

---

## 1. YC Structure (10-12 Slides)

**When to use:** Early-stage (Pre-Seed, Seed), demo days, cold outreach to angels and
early-stage VCs. Best when you have strong traction but limited time or attention span.
Ideal for email-first decks that must communicate value in under 60 seconds of scanning.

**Total recommended slide count:** 10 core, up to 12 with optional slides.

| # | Slide Type | Purpose | Key Content | Design Emphasis |
|---|-----------|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Cover** | Establish identity and create instant clarity on what the company does. | Company name, one-liner tagline (max 8 words), logo, optional founding year. | Full-bleed brand color or hero image. Logo centered. No clutter. The one-liner must pass the "billboard test" -- readable at a glance. |
| 2 | **Problem** | Make the audience feel the pain before you offer the cure. | Describe the problem in human terms. Quantify the pain (dollars lost, hours wasted, people affected). Use a concrete persona or scenario. | Single powerful stat or quote as the visual anchor. Minimal text. Consider a brief real-world vignette. Dark or high-contrast background to set tension. |
| 3 | **Solution** | State what you built, not how you built it. | One sentence value proposition. Screenshot or product visual. Outcome-oriented framing ("X gets Y without Z"). | Product screenshot or mockup front and center. Avoid technical architecture. The visual must answer "what does the user see?" |
| 4 | **Traction** | Prove momentum with the single strongest metric. | Lead metric: MRR/ARR, growth rate (MoM or YoY), user count, retention rate. Secondary metric optional. Time-series chart showing upward trajectory. | Large number or chart dominating the slide. Use a hockey-stick or steep-growth line. Green/positive color for the metric. No vanity metrics. |
| 5 | **How It Works** | Demystify the product in three steps. | 3-step product flow with icons or mini-screenshots. Each step: verb + outcome (e.g., "Connect your data source", "AI analyzes patterns", "Get actionable alerts"). | Horizontal 3-column layout or numbered flow. Icons or small visuals per step. Keep each step to one line. |
| 6 | **Market Size** | Show the opportunity is large enough to matter. | Bottom-up TAM/SAM/SOM calculation. Start from number of target customers x average revenue per customer. Cite sources. Avoid top-down "the market is $50B" without derivation. | Concentric circles or funnel diagram for TAM/SAM/SOM. Bold the SOM number -- that is what you are actually targeting. |
| 7 | **Business Model** | Explain how you make money and why the economics work. | Revenue model (SaaS, marketplace, usage-based). Pricing tiers if applicable. Unit economics: CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC ratio, gross margin. | Simple pricing table or unit economics summary. Highlight LTV:CAC ratio. If usage-based, show expansion revenue potential. |
| 8 | **Team** | Build credibility through the people behind the product. | 3-5 key team members. Name, role, one credential line each (relevant prior company, domain expertise, technical background). Headshots. | Grid layout with professional headshots. One line per person. Highlight domain-relevant experience, not generic bios. Logos of notable prior employers if applicable. |
| 9 | **Competition** | Show you understand the landscape and where you win. | 2x2 matrix (axes relevant to your differentiation) or feature comparison table. Position yourself in the top-right quadrant. Name real competitors. | 2x2 matrix preferred. Your logo in the winning quadrant. Competitor logos in other quadrants. Avoid listing 15 competitors -- pick the 4-6 that matter. |
| 10 | **The Ask** | State exactly what you need and what you will do with it. | Funding amount, round type, use of funds breakdown (3-4 buckets), key milestones the funding unlocks (with timeline). | Pie chart or bar chart for fund allocation. Milestones as a simple timeline below. Bold the total ask amount. |
| 11 | **Financials** *(optional)* | Provide forward-looking projections for investors who want depth. | 3-year revenue projection, key assumptions, path to profitability or next fundraise. Monthly burn rate. | Simple line or bar chart. Label assumptions clearly. Avoid overly optimistic projections without justification. |
| 12 | **Vision** *(optional)* | End with an aspirational view of the future you are building. | Long-term mission statement. Where the company is in 5-10 years. The world you want to create. | Bold statement on a clean background. Inspirational but grounded. No generic "change the world" -- be specific to your domain. |

## 2. Sequoia Structure (15-20 Slides)

**When to use:** Series A and beyond. When you have a compelling narrative arc and need
to build a thorough case. Best for warm introductions to top-tier VCs who will spend
15-20 minutes with your deck. The Sequoia structure rewards storytelling and historical context.

**Total recommended slide count:** 13 core, up to 20 with appendix material.

| # | Slide Type | Purpose | Key Content | Design Emphasis |
|---|-----------|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Cover** | Set the stage with brand presence and clarity. | Company name, logo, tagline, presenter name, date. Optional: stage of funding. | Clean, confident design. Brand colors. No visual noise. |
| 2 | **Company Purpose** | Distill the entire company into one declarative sentence. | One sentence that captures why the company exists. Not a product description -- a mission statement. Example: "We organize the world's restaurant supply chain." | Large text, centered. This slide has no charts, no images -- just the sentence. Let it breathe. Serif or display font for gravitas. |
| 3 | **Problem** | Create emotional resonance and intellectual understanding of the pain. | Who experiences the problem, how they currently cope, what it costs them (time, money, risk). Use data and a human story. | Split layout: data on one side, human element (quote, photo, scenario) on the other. Build tension. |
| 4 | **Solution** | Present your value proposition as the answer to the problem. | What you offer, primary benefit, how it differs from current alternatives. Outcome-focused language. | Product visual or conceptual diagram. Connect visually to the problem slide -- show the "before and after." |
| 5 | **Why Now** | Explain the historical forces that make this the right moment. This is Sequoia's signature slide. | Technology shifts, regulatory changes, cultural trends, cost curve crossings, pandemic effects, demographic shifts. Show the timeline of what changed. | **(SIGNATURE SLIDE)** Timeline or inflection-point chart. Show 3-5 converging trends. This slide should make the investor think "of course, the timing is perfect." Use a visual that shows acceleration or convergence. |
| 6 | **Market Size** | Quantify the opportunity with rigorous bottom-up math. | TAM/SAM/SOM with bottom-up derivation. Number of target customers x ARPU. Growth rate of the market. Cite credible sources (Gartner, internal data, public filings). | Layered diagram (concentric circles or stacked bars). Show the math, not just the number. Include a footnote with methodology. |
| 7 | **Product** | Showcase the product in detail, including roadmap. | Current features with screenshots or demo frames. Product roadmap (next 12-18 months). Key differentiating capabilities. Platform/ecosystem view if applicable. | Multi-screenshot layout or annotated product walkthrough. Roadmap as a horizontal timeline with shipped vs. planned. |
| 8 | **Business Model** | Demonstrate a clear and scalable path to revenue. | Revenue model, pricing structure, unit economics (CAC, LTV, gross margin, payback period). Expansion revenue mechanics. | Unit economics table or waterfall chart. Highlight gross margin and LTV:CAC. If marketplace: show take rate and GMV. |
| 9 | **Competition** | Map the competitive landscape and your positioning. | Competitive landscape with positioning. Why incumbents cannot easily replicate you. Where you win and where you choose not to compete. | Landscape map (2x2 or radar chart). Include both direct and indirect competitors. Show your unfair advantage clearly. |
| 10 | **Team** | Demonstrate founder-market fit and organizational strength. | Founders with deep bios (2-3 lines each). Key hires and advisors. Board members if notable. Highlight domain expertise and prior exits. | More detailed than YC format. Include advisory board. Show org chart if team is 20+. Emphasize why THIS team for THIS problem. |
| 11 | **Financials** | Present historical performance and forward projections. | Historical P&L (if available), 3-year projections, key assumptions, burn rate, runway, capital efficiency metrics. | Table or chart format. Show actuals vs. prior projections to build credibility. Highlight path to break-even or next raise. |
| 12 | **Vision** | Paint the long-term picture and the company's enduring mission. | 5-10 year vision. Category creation potential. Platform evolution. How the world looks different if you succeed. | Aspirational visual or bold statement. Connect back to the Company Purpose slide -- show the full arc. |
| 13 | **The Ask** | Close with a clear, specific funding request. | Amount, round terms (if sharing), use of funds, 18-month milestones, what success looks like at next fundraise. | Clean layout. Milestone timeline prominent. Fund allocation breakdown. End with confidence, not a question. |
| 14-20 | **Appendix** *(optional)* | Provide depth for due diligence without cluttering the narrative. | Case studies with named customers and ROI data. Detailed financial model assumptions. Technical architecture (for deep-tech). Cohort analyses. Patent portfolio. Press coverage. | Clearly labeled as "Appendix." Each slide self-contained. Numbered for easy reference in follow-up conversations. |

## 3. a16z Structure (12-15 Slides)

**When to use:** When your company is built on a non-obvious insight or a technical moat.
Best for companies with strong product-market fit signals, defensible technology, or
network effects. Ideal for Series A/B where differentiation and defensibility matter
more than raw growth.

**Total recommended slide count:** 11 core, up to 15 with supporting slides.

| # | Slide Type | Purpose | Key Content | Design Emphasis |
|---|-----------|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Cover** | Brand presence and framing. | Company name, logo, tagline, category label (e.g., "AI-native supply chain intelligence"). | Modern, bold design. Consider a category-defining subtitle. |
| 2 | **Big Idea / Thesis** | Declare the fundamental shift that enables your company. | One macro thesis about how the world is changing. Frame the structural shift -- technology, behavior, regulation -- that creates the opening. | Bold statement format. Think "newspaper headline from the future." This is not your product -- it is the wave you are riding. |
| 3 | **Problem** | Ground the thesis in real suffering and real cost. | Who suffers, what it costs them, how often, and why existing solutions fail. Quantify with data. | Data-driven. Use a stark statistic as the visual anchor. Show the gap between "what is" and "what should be." |
| 4 | **Unique Insight** | Reveal what you know that the market does not. This is a16z's signature slide. | A proprietary insight, counterintuitive truth, or discovered pattern that gives you an unfair starting advantage. Not a feature -- a worldview. | **(SIGNATURE SLIDE)** This slide must make the investor pause. Frame it as a "secret" or "what we discovered." Use contrast (conventional wisdom vs. your insight). Minimal text, maximum impact. |
| 5 | **Product / Demo** | Show the product, do not just describe it. | Live screenshots, demo video stills, or interactive prototype frames. Annotate key moments. Show the user experience, not the backend. | Visual-heavy. Annotated screenshots or a 4-6 frame product walkthrough. If video demo exists, embed a QR code to it. |
| 6 | **Traction** | Prove product-market fit with engagement depth, not just growth. | Engagement metrics (DAU/MAU ratio), retention curves (cohort analysis), NPS, usage frequency. Revenue traction secondary to engagement proof. | Cohort retention chart is the star. Show improving cohorts over time. Include DAU/MAU ratio. Highlight "smile curve" retention if applicable. |
| 7 | **Go-to-Market** | Describe your specific, executable GTM strategy. | Named channels with economics. Sales motion (PLG, enterprise, hybrid). Current pipeline or waitlist. Customer acquisition playbook, not aspirational statements. | Funnel diagram with conversion rates at each stage. Or a GTM motion diagram showing the specific flywheel. Avoid generic "we will do content marketing." |
| 8 | **Market** | Size the opportunity with bottom-up rigor. | Bottom-up market sizing. Current penetration rate. Expansion vectors (new segments, geographies, products). Growth drivers. | Bottom-up math visible. Show your assumptions. Highlight the wedge you are entering through and the expansion potential. |
| 9 | **Team** | Explain founder-market fit -- why THIS team for THIS problem. | Founders with specific domain credentials. Key insight or experience that led to the unique insight. Prior relevant exits or domain expertise. | Narrative format preferred over grid. Connect each founder's background to the problem and insight. Show the "unfair advantage" in human capital. |
| 10 | **Moats** | Enumerate your defensibility layers. This is a16z's second signature element. | Network effects (and what kind), switching costs, proprietary data, regulatory barriers, technical complexity, brand, economies of scale. Be specific. | **(SIGNATURE SLIDE)** Moat diagram or layered defense visual. Show how moats compound over time. Be honest about which moats exist today vs. which you are building. |
| 11 | **The Ask** | Close with amount, milestones, and what the money buys in terms of moat-building. | Funding amount, milestone targets (tied to moat deepening), timeline, what triggers the next round. | Milestones framed around defensibility growth, not just revenue. Show how funding deepens the moats on slide 10. |
| 12-15 | **Supporting Slides** *(optional)* | Depth for specific investor questions. | Detailed unit economics, financial projections, technical architecture, IP portfolio, regulatory strategy. | Appendix-style. Reference-ready for follow-up meetings. |

## 4. 2026 Extended Variant

**When to use:** Apply these additions to ANY of the three base structures above.
These slides reflect 2026 investor expectations and are considered mandatory for
Series A and above. For Seed/Pre-Seed, include at minimum the AI Strategy slide.

**How to integrate:** Insert these slides into the base structure at the positions noted below.
They do not replace existing slides -- they augment the deck.

### AI Strategy Slide

**Insert after:** Product slide (or after Business Model if AI is the business model).

| Aspect | Specification |
|--------|--------------|
| **Purpose** | Demonstrate how AI creates or deepens competitive advantage, regardless of whether the company is an "AI company." |
| **Key Content** | AI's role in the product or operations. Proprietary data advantages. Model strategy (build vs. fine-tune vs. API). AI cost structure and trajectory. How AI improves with scale (data flywheel). Responsible AI practices. |
| **Design Emphasis** | Diagram showing the AI feedback loop or data flywheel. Avoid generic "we use AI" statements. Show specifics: what model, what data, what improvement curve. Include cost per inference or AI-driven margin improvement if applicable. |
| **Required regardless of sector** | Even non-AI companies must address: How does AI affect your industry? How are you using AI operationally? What is your AI risk mitigation strategy? |

### Unit Economics Slide

**Insert after:** Business Model slide.

| Aspect | Specification |
|--------|--------------|
| **Purpose** | Prove capital efficiency and sustainable growth economics. Investors in 2026 demand proof of efficient growth, not growth at all costs. |
| **Key Content** | Net Dollar Retention (NDR) -- target >120% for SaaS. CAC Payback Period -- target <18 months. Burn Multiple (net burn / net new ARR) -- target <2x. LTV:CAC ratio -- target >3:1. Gross margin trajectory. Magic number or similar efficiency metric. |
| **Design Emphasis** | Dashboard-style layout with each metric as a card or gauge. Use RAG (Red/Amber/Green) indicators against benchmarks. Show trend lines, not just current values. Compare to industry medians if favorable. |

### ESG / Mission Slide

**Insert after:** Vision slide (or before The Ask).

| Aspect | Specification |
|--------|--------------|
| **Purpose** | Address growing investor expectations around environmental, social, and governance factors. Increasingly expected in European fundraises and by LPs of major funds. |
| **Key Content** | Environmental: Carbon footprint measurement, reduction targets, sustainable infrastructure choices. Social: D&I metrics (team composition), pay equity practices, community impact. Governance: Board composition, data ethics policies, transparency practices. |
| **Design Emphasis** | Clean, factual presentation. Avoid greenwashing. Show measurable commitments with timelines. Certifications and frameworks (B-Corp, UN SDGs) if applicable. For European investors, reference EU taxonomy alignment if relevant. |

## Structure Selection Matrix

| Scenario | Recommended Structure | Rationale |
|----------|----------------------|-----------|
| YC Demo Day or batch application | YC | Brevity is mandatory. Traction-first. |
| Cold email to Seed/Pre-Seed VC | YC | Must communicate value in 60 seconds of scanning. |
| Warm intro to top-tier Series A VC | Sequoia | Narrative depth rewards the attention you have earned. |
| Deep-tech or insight-driven company | a16z | Your edge is the insight and the moat, not just growth. |
| European / DACH fundraise | Sequoia + 2026 Extended | European investors expect thoroughness and ESG. |
| Growth round (Series B+) | Sequoia or a16z + 2026 Extended | Metrics-heavy with defensibility narrative. |
| AI-native company | a16z + 2026 Extended | Insight + moat framing is natural for AI. |
| Board presentation (existing investors) | See AUDIENCE_GUIDE.md | Different format entirely -- not a pitch deck. |
